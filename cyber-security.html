Ethical Hacker and Cyber Security

Learn more about this role

Ethical hacking, also known as penetration testing or pen testing, is legally breaking into computers and devices to test an organization's defenses. It's among the most exciting IT jobs any person can be involved in. You are literally getting paid to keep up with the latest technology and get to break into computers without the threat of being arrested.

    A ethical hackers role can include:
  • Using port scanning tools like Nmap or Nessus to scan an organization’s systems and find open ports. The vulnerabilities with each of these ports can be studied and remedial measures can be taken.
  • Examining security patch installations and making sure that they can’t be exploited.
  • Employing other social engineering techniques like shoulder surfing to gain access to crucial information or playing the kindness card to trick employees to part with their passwords.
  • Understand software, hardware, and user interactions, integrations, and interfaces.
  • seeking to build in security during the development stages of software systems, networks and data centres
